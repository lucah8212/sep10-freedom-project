About

Coming up with the idea for the PilotVision Glasses came from research done on the technology that we have today in aviation, from software that assists pilots, to hardware as complex as collision-avoidance systems.


Things that Exist today


Software

  • Garmin Pilot

    • Allows pilots to plan flights on their computer from wherever, keep their logbook up to date (when they flew, how many hours, etc.), and access exclusive weather features. Garmin Pilot also provides full en route navigation on the dynamic moving map or on charts.

  • ForeFlight

    • Pilots and flight crews across the world depend on ForeFlight to make flying safer, easier, and more efficient. Real-time charts are helpful for pilots, especially during departure and arrival stages of the flights, helping give sense of where they are.

Both of these apps serve the main purpose of navigation for pilots, along with weather briefings and a variety of charts

Hardware

  • Flight Computer

    • Performs critical aviation computations, including time, speed, distance, heading, wind, fuel, altitude, and more. Integrated calculator which solves multiple flight-related problems in a single function.

  • Collision Avoidance

    • Collision advoidance systems like the Traffic Collision Avoidance System (TCAS) exist in airplanes to help prevent mid-air collisions by detecting nearby aircraft equipped with transponders. It alerts pilots with visual and audio cues if another aircraft is too close and may pose a collision risk. This system has helped prevent thousands of mid air collisions.
